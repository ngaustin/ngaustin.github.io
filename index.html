My Research

Research

My research focuses on multi-agent reinforcement learning and game theoretic approaches that allow agents to learn equilibria in both cooperative and competitive environments. I am grateful to be advised by Professor Michael Wellman.

Ant Colonies and Multi-Agent Coordination

Inspired by ant behavior, I combined learning with a pheromone-based multi-agent coordination algorithm that allowed fleets of automobiles to learn how to navigate to a goal location. End-to-end, they had to learn how to use large boxes to make new paths, choose which paths to take, which boxes/holes to use, and how to avoid collisions with each other.

Game Theoretic MARL for Cooperation

Currently, I am looking into how to multi-agent reinforcement learning grounded in game theory to help solve cooperative games. The issue I am addressing is how to find pareto-optimal solutions in games where multiple equilibria are present.
