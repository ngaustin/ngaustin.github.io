My Research

I work in the intersection of multi-agent reinforcement learning and game theory. I primarily devise game-solving algorithms that minimize computation and data resources. I believe there are several hurdles that need to be addressed before multi-agent learning systems can be introduced and assimilated into the real world, of which I hope to address during my time at the University of Michigan.

My research works to expand the reliability, efficiency, and efficacy of multi-agent learning algorithms and ground them in game-theoretic principles.
